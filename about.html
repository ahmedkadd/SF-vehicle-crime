About

Vehicle crime is a massive problem in San Francisco. Car break-ins and auto theft are extremely common, and the problem has gotten worse in recent years. The goal of this project is to educate people on where and when vehicle crime is most likely to occur. I used San Francisco Police Department data to create visualizations that examine the problem. I would like to answer the questions:

  1. Where are the most dangerous places to park your car in San Francisco?
  2. When is the most dangerous time to park your car in public

By answering these questions, I hope this project can be utilized as a tool by San Francisco residents to make safer and more informed decisions with where they leave their cars. I also chose to visualize this data because San Francisco vehicle is a problem that has affected my family, my friends, and me. I have had my car broken into with luggage I had packed for a trip inside. When I got back to my car, all the luggage was stolen. I filed a police report, but nothing came out of it. This project is meant to bring more attention to this problem, and to see the trends and areas in which it is most common. I hope this tool will help people think about where they park their car and what they leave in it to reduce the number of incidents.

This website also highlights the process I went through to develop the data visualizations. The visualizations are updated to show the last 30 months of data. You can find information on the data set on the data tab, see my alpha and beta prototypes on the prototypes tab, then view the final product on the visualizations tab.
